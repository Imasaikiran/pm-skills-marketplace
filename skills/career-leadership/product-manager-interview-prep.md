# Product Manager Interview Prep

## Description
Comprehensive guide to preparing for and succeeding in product manager interviews, covering all major question types and evaluation criteria.

## Interview Types

### 1. Product Design Questions
**Format:** "How would you design X?"

**Examples:**
- Design a parking app for SF
- Improve Instagram Stories
- Create a food delivery service for seniors

**Framework (Circles Method):**
1. **Comprehend:** Clarify the question
2. **Identify:** Customer segments
3. **Report:** Customer needs
4. **Cut:** Prioritize key need
5. **List:** Solutions
6. **Evaluate:** Trade-offs
7. **Summarize:** Recommendation

### 2. Estimation Questions
**Format:** "How many/much X?"

**Examples:**
- How many iPhones sold in US last year?
- What's the market size for pet grooming?
- Estimate Uber's daily rides in NYC

**Approach:**
- Break into components
- Make reasonable assumptions (state them!)
- Show math clearly
- Sanity check result

### 3. Strategy Questions
**Format:** Company/product strategy decisions

**Examples:**
- Should Netflix enter gaming?
- How would you grow Twitter users?
- What should Spotify's next priority be?

**Framework:**
- Clarify objective
- Analyze current state
- Identify options
- Evaluate trade-offs
- Make recommendation

### 4. Behavioral Questions
**Format:** Tell me about a time...

**Examples:**
- Led a cross-functional team
- Dealt with difficult stakeholder
- Failed at something
- Made a data-driven decision

**STAR Method:**
- **S**ituation: Set context
- **T**ask: Your role/challenge
- **A**ction: What you did
- **R**esult: Outcome and learning

### 5. Technical Questions
**Format:** System design or technical trade-offs

**Examples:**
- Design a URL shortener
- How would you scale a messaging system?
- SQL vs NoSQL for our use case?

**Approach:**
- Start with requirements
- Discuss architecture options
- Consider scale/performance
- Show technical judgment

### 6. Metrics Questions
**Format:** How would you measure X?

**Examples:**
- Success metrics for new feature
- Diagnose drop in engagement
- Track marketplace health

**Framework:**
- Define goal
- Identify key metrics
- Explain leading vs lagging
- Describe how to measure
- Discuss trade-offs

### 7. Execution Questions
**Format:** How would you build/launch X?

**Examples:**
- Launch a new payment method
- Roll out a redesign
- Handle a failed feature

**Approach:**
- Planning & scoping
- Execution strategy
- Risk mitigation
- Success criteria
- Communication plan

## Common PM Interview Questions

### Product Sense
1. What's your favorite product and why?
2. How would you improve [company's product]?
3. Design a product for [specific user need]
4. What makes a great product?
5. Walk me through a product you shipped

### Leadership & Influence
1. Tell me about a time you influenced without authority
2. How do you handle disagreements with engineers?
3. Describe a time you had to make an unpopular decision
4. How do you prioritize when stakeholders disagree?
5. Tell me about a failed project

### Analytical Thinking
1. How would you measure success for [feature]?
2. Our engagement dropped 10% - what do you do?
3. Estimate the TAM for [product]
4. How do you make decisions without complete data?
5. Walk me through an A/B test you ran

### Technical Acumen
1. How do APIs work?
2. Explain [technical concept] to a non-technical person
3. What questions do you ask engineers?
4. How do you evaluate technical trade-offs?
5. Describe your ideal tech stack for [use case]

## Interview Day Tips

### Before Interview
- Review company and role
- Test video setup (if remote)
- Prepare notebook and pen
- Have questions ready
- Plan to arrive 10 minutes early

### During Interview
- Think out loud
- Ask clarifying questions
- Structure your answers
- Be enthusiastic
- Show humility
- Listen actively

### After Interview
- Send thank you email (within 24 hours)
- Reflect on what went well/poorly
- Note questions for next time

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ Not asking clarifying questions
❌ Jumping to solution without understanding
❌ Only feature-focused, no user empathy
❌ Can't explain past decisions
❌ Blaming others for failures
❌ No questions for interviewer
❌ Not structured in approach
❌ Overly negative about past companies

## Example Answer: Product Design

**Question:** "Design a product for college students to find study partners"

**Answer (Circles Method):**

**Comprehend:**
"To clarify - are we designing for a specific school or nationwide? Any constraints on platform?"
[Get answers]

**Identify Customers:**
Main segments:
- Freshmen (new, seeking friends)
- Upperclassmen (focused on grades)
- STEM vs Humanities (different study needs)

**Report Needs:**
- Find compatible study partners
- Coordinate meeting times/locations
- Share notes and resources
- Track study sessions
- Build accountability

**Cut (Prioritize):**
Focus on: "Find compatible study partners quickly"
Why: Biggest friction point, unlocks other features

**List Solutions:**
1. Matching algorithm by course/schedule
2. Study group forums
3. 1-on-1 flashcard battles
4. Library map showing who's studying what

**Evaluate:**
Go with #1: Matching algorithm
- Highest value (direct solution)
- Easiest to build (existing patterns)
- Network effects

**Summarize:**
Build a Tinder-style app where students:
- Input courses and availability
- See potential study partners
- Match based on schedule/location/learning style
- Chat to coordinate sessions

MVP: Course input + basic matching + chat
Future: Group study, note sharing, reputation system

## Questions to Ask Interviewer

**About Role:**
- What does success look like in first 90 days?
- What are biggest challenges for PM team?
- How is product team structured?

**About Product:**
- What's on roadmap for next 6 months?
- How do you balance tech debt vs features?
- What's the product vision?

**About Culture:**
- How do PMs collaborate with eng/design?
- What does PM career growth look like?
- How are decisions made?
