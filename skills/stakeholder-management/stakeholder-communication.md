# Stakeholder Communication

## Description
Effectively communicate with stakeholders to align on goals, manage expectations, gather input, and build support for product decisions.

## When to Use
- Sharing product updates
- Gathering requirements
- Managing expectations
- Building alignment
- Reporting progress
- Handling conflicts

## Types of Stakeholders

### Internal Stakeholders
**Executives (CEO, CTO, CFO)**
- Care about: Business impact, ROI, strategy
- Communication style: Brief, data-driven, outcomes-focused
- Frequency: Monthly/Quarterly

**Engineering Team**
- Care about: Technical feasibility, architecture, workload
- Communication style: Detailed, collaborative, honest
- Frequency: Daily/Weekly

**Sales & Marketing**
- Care about: Customer impact, competitive advantage, launch dates
- Communication style: Clear positioning, timelines, benefits
- Frequency: Weekly/Bi-weekly

**Customer Success/Support**
- Care about: User experience, customer feedback, training needs
- Communication style: Empathetic, practical, solution-oriented
- Frequency: Weekly

**Design Team**
- Care about: User experience, design system, creative freedom
- Communication style: Collaborative, visual, user-focused
- Frequency: Daily/Weekly

### External Stakeholders
**Customers/Users**
- Care about: Value, usability, solving their problems
- Communication: Transparent, user-benefit focused
- Channels: Email, in-app, blog, community

**Partners**
- Care about: Integration, mutual benefit, timelines
- Communication: Professional, clear, reliable
- Frequency: As needed

## Communication Framework

### The 5 W's + H

**What:** What are you communicating?
- Product update
- Decision made
- Input needed
- Status report

**Why:** Why does it matter?
- Business impact
- User value
- Strategic importance

**Who:** Who needs to know?
- Primary audience
- Secondary audience
- Who's affected

**When:** When do they need to know?
- Urgency level
- Timing considerations
- Frequency

**Where:** What channel?
- Email, Slack, meeting
- Document, presentation
- Dashboard, report

**How:** How should you communicate?
- Tone and style
- Level of detail
- Format

## Communication Channels

### Synchronous (Real-time)

**Meetings**
- Best for: Complex discussions, decisions, alignment
- When: Important topics, multiple stakeholders
- Duration: 30-60 minutes max

**Slack/Teams**
- Best for: Quick questions, updates, coordination
- When: Time-sensitive, informal
- Response time: Minutes to hours

**Video Calls**
- Best for: Face-to-face connection, demos, workshops
- When: Remote collaboration, relationship building

### Asynchronous

**Email**
- Best for: Formal communication, announcements, summaries
- When: Important updates, broad audience
- Response time: 24-48 hours

**Documents**
- Best for: Detailed information, references, specs
- When: Complex topics, need for documentation
- Access: Ongoing

**Dashboards**
- Best for: Metrics, progress tracking, transparency
- When: Regular updates, data sharing
- Update frequency: Real-time to weekly

## Managing Difficult Conversations

### Saying No to Features

**Framework:**
1. **Acknowledge:** "I understand this is important to you"
2. **Explain:** Share the reasoning (data, strategy, priorities)
3. **Offer Alternative:** What can you do instead?
4. **Timeline:** When might this be reconsidered?

**Example:**
"I understand the sales team really wants custom branding. Given our current focus on retention (where we're seeing 25% churn), we need to prioritize features that keep existing users engaged. What if we start with basic theme options this quarter and revisit full customization in Q3?"

### Delivering Bad News

**Framework:**
1. **Direct:** Lead with the news, don't bury it
2. **Context:** Explain what happened
3. **Impact:** Clarify how this affects them
4. **Plan:** Share next steps
5. **Support:** Offer to discuss

**Example:**
"The mobile app launch is delayed by 3 weeks. During testing, we discovered performance issues that affect user experience. This means the launch date moves from March 15 to April 5. I know this impacts the campaign timing. Let's discuss how to adjust the marketing plan."

### Handling Conflicting Priorities

**Framework:**
1. **Surface the conflict:** Make it explicit
2. **Clarify criteria:** What matters most?
3. **Present options:** Trade-offs for each choice
4. **Facilitate decision:** Help stakeholders decide
5. **Document:** Record decision and rationale

## Meeting Best Practices

### Before the Meeting

- [ ] **Clear agenda** sent 24 hours ahead
- [ ] **Relevant materials** shared for review
- [ ] **Right attendees** invited (no more than 8)
- [ ] **Time allocated** appropriately
- [ ] **Desired outcome** defined

### During the Meeting

- [ ] **Start on time** (respect people's calendars)
- [ ] **State purpose** in first 2 minutes
- [ ] **Stay on topic** (parking lot for tangents)
- [ ] **Capture decisions** and action items
- [ ] **Confirm next steps** before ending
- [ ] **End on time** (or early!)

### After the Meeting

- [ ] **Send notes** within 24 hours
- [ ] **List action items** with owners and dates
- [ ] **Document decisions** and rationale
- [ ] **Follow up** on commitments

## Building Stakeholder Relationships

### Regular Touchpoints

**Weekly:**
- Engineering standups
- Design sync
- Support ticket review

**Bi-weekly:**
- Sales/Marketing sync
- Customer feedback review

**Monthly:**
- Executive update
- Roadmap review with stakeholders
- Cross-functional planning

**Quarterly:**
- Strategy review
- OKR planning
- Retrospectives

### Building Trust

**Be Transparent:**
- Share challenges, not just wins
- Admit when you don't know
- Explain trade-offs honestly

**Follow Through:**
- Do what you say you'll do
- Meet deadlines
- Keep people updated

**Listen Actively:**
- Ask questions
- Seek to understand
- Acknowledge concerns

**Give Credit:**
- Recognize contributions
- Celebrate team wins
- Share success broadly

## Influence Without Authority

### Techniques

**1. Build Coalition**
- Find allies who support your idea
- Create groundswell before formal pitch
- Use peer influence

**2. Use Data**
- Show customer impact
- Present market research
- Share metrics and trends

**3. Tell Stories**
- User anecdotes
- Competitive examples
- Vision of the future

**4. Start Small**
- Pilot or experiment
- Prove value incrementally
- Build momentum

**5. Frame Properly**
- Align with their goals
- Speak their language
- Address their concerns

## Crisis Communication

### When Things Go Wrong

**Immediate Actions (First Hour):**
1. Assess situation
2. Identify affected stakeholders
3. Draft initial communication
4. Communicate what you know
5. Promise updates

**Template:**
```markdown
Subject: [Product] Issue - [Brief Description]

## What Happened
[Clear description]

## Who's Affected
[Users, features, scope]

## What We're Doing
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]

## When We'll Update
[Next update time]

## How to Help
[If applicable]
```

**Follow-up Communication:**
- Regular updates (even if "no news")
- Clear resolution timeline
- Post-mortem when resolved

## Communication Anti-Patterns

### ‚ùå Surprises
- Sharing bad news last minute
- Solution: Communicate early and often

### ‚ùå Jargon Overload
- Using PM-specific terms
- Solution: Use clear, plain language

### ‚ùå One-Way Updates
- Just broadcasting information
- Solution: Invite questions and feedback

### ‚ùå Over-Communicating
- Too many channels, too much detail
- Solution: Be concise, use right channel

### ‚ùå Under-Communicating
- Radio silence, then big reveals
- Solution: Regular cadence of updates
